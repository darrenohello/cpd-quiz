## 7B. The correct answer is: D) May 31

The Nursing and Midwifery Board of Australia’s registration period is 1 June - 31 May. This means that the CPD deadline for nurses and midwives is 31 May each year.

## 8B. CPD hours

The total CPD hours required for nurses and midwives is different according to your profession and registration type. Additional CPD will be required if you hold a dual registration (i.e. as a registered midwife and nurse) or if you hold any endorsements (for example, as a nurse practitioner). 

Generally, a minimum of 20 hours is required. If you have an endorsement there will be an additional minimum 10 hours requirement per endorsement.

## 9. True or false: Pro-rata CPD is only relevant to newly graduated students

A) True
B) False
C) Not sure

## 9B.The correct answer is: False

Pro-rata CPD is relevant to any practitioner who has held a registration for less than 12 months prior to renewal of registration. This is commonly a student nurse or midwife but also applies to a practitioner who has changed their registration type during a registration period. For example, a practitioner who has moved from a practicing to non-practicing registration type.  

Additionally, pro-rata CPD requirements are relevant to a practitioner who in the registration period has gained an endorsement. Think, a recently upskilled nurse who has become a nurse practitioner. 

## 10. True or false: Your context of practice is the same as your scope of practice

A) True
B) False
C) Not sure

## 10B. The correct answer is: False

**Context of practice is not the same as scope of practice. **

Context of practice refers to the conditions that define an individual’s practice. These include the setting, population, primary focus and complexity of care provided. Scope of practice is ‘the professional role and services that an individual health practitioner is trained, qualified and competent to perform’. It can be thought of as acting within laws and regulations, and providing care depending on your certification and competency. 

Your scope of practice will depend on the context of your practice, which will change based on population, setting and complexity levels. CPD must be relevant to a practitioner’s identified context of practice. 

## 11. True or false: All mandatory training can count towards your CPD.

A) True
B) False
C) Not sure

## 11B. The correct answer is: False

Mandatory training activities in the workplace may be counted as CPD provided they are relevant to your context of practice and include new learning.

You must be able to demonstrate that they improve your knowledge, expertise and competence and relate to an identified learning goal. 

## 12. Do you complete CPD throughout the CPD year? 

A) Yes, I usually complete CPD throughout the year
B) No, I tend to leave it to the CPD deadline

## 12B. Completing CPD throughout the CPD year

Did you know that the NMBA needs you to engage in CPD activities throughout a registration period? This means you are encouraged to spread your CPD activities over the course of the year. 

This is because learning and development occurs throughout your career. CPD is a critical foundation of lifelong learning and helps nurses and midwives maintain their competence to practice.

## 15B. Learning Plans

Research on CPD shows that planning CPD results in positive learning outcomes and evidence-based changes to practice.

This means that CPD is more effective when it involves planning and reflection of learning goals. You will get the most benefit from your CPD activities by identifying your learning goals and planning relevant activities to meet these goals. Planning your CPD is a great opportunity to review best-practice and self-evaluate it against your current knowledge, skills and competency.

A) Strongly agree
B) Agree
C) Neither agree nor disagree

## 16B. Reviewing learning goals at the end of each CPD year 

Reflection on your learning, including what you have achieved and applied to practice, is a helpful activity at the end of each CPD year. This is because unmet learning goals from one year may contribute to your learning goals for the coming year as part of the ongoing CPD cycle. 

Regular reviews of your learning plan and identified learning goals can also help ensure that your CPD is effective at contributing to increasing your knowledge, skills and current context of practice. An actual or planned change in role, for example, a promotion or new job, is another great opportunity to review your learning goals and plan CPD activities to support this change. 

## 17. True or false: Certificates are the only the evidence I am required to show if I am audited for CPD

A) True
B) False
C) Not sure

## 17B. The correct answer is: False

You must keep records of your CPD activities and this evidence must be available if you are audited.

A certificate of attendance or attainment is a format of evidence accepted by the NMBA. However, evidence of your CPD must also include your documentation of the identified learning need, a learning plan, your participation in the learning activity, and a reflection on the outcome achieved, including how the activity relates to your practice.

## 19. When do you confirm you have met all your CPD requirements?

A) During a performance appraisal
B) On my CV
C) At renewal of registration
D) At the end of each calendar year
E) I’m not sure

## 19B. The correct answer is: C) At renewal of registration

Each year, at the renewal of registration, which is when you apply to renew your registration and/or endorsement, you are required to declare that you have complied with the registration standard: continuing professional development.

It is therefore essential to understand all of the requirements outlined in the registration standard, such as planning, reflection and activities being relevant to your context of practice - not just the amount of CPD hours you are required to complete.

## 20. How many years do you need to keep records of CPD for?

A) 7 years
B) 3 years
C) 5 years
D) 10 years

## 20B. The correct answer is: C) 5 years

You must keep records of your CPD activities for a period of 5 years from the date you completed the CPD.

This may be required if you are audited or as part of an investigation should a matter arise from a notification about you (a complaint).
